Insight

  • 1961 - 2019

    Food Production

    We noticed that while the labor in agricultural production was decreasing over the years the gross output was increasing that indicated an increase in food productivity. We also found a significant shift in major food suppliers from industrialized countries to developing countries.

  • 1961 - 2021

    Trade and Price

    Although the trade volume has been always increasing and the world GDP has been going up, the annual growth rate has not fully recovered after the financial crisis. This impacted the consumer price to not increase much before the pandemic, yet the world inflation went up during the pandemic and it led the consumer price increase 15 percent more.

  • 2019 - 2021

    Rising Food Prices World-Wide Amid COVID-19

    Different factors including lockdowns and the breakdown of local food supply contribute to the rise of prices. This visualization shows the extent of price increases for certain food commodities in different countries from 2019 to July 2021.

  • 2019 - 2021

    Correlation between pandemic and food economy

    We found that for the dependent variables of Food balance and imports, Covid has little correlation and impact for either.
